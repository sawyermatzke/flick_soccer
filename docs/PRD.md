# PRD

## Product
A real-money-targeted App Store iOS multiplayer game that faithfully recreates the feel and ruleset of Binho-style tabletop flick soccer, while architecting branding, theme, and content so the product can be re-skinned into an original identity if legal, commercial, or platform considerations require it.

## Objectives
- Ship a polished iOS game.
- MVP includes live multiplayer.
- Core gameplay should feel skill-based, readable, and satisfying on touch devices.
- Branding/theme assets must be swappable without rewriting the gameplay layer.

## Non-goals for MVP
- Android launch
- ranked ladder at launch unless it falls out naturally
- heavy progression systems
- large cosmetics catalog
- assumption-heavy physical board reconstruction without evidence

## MVP
- iPhone-first app
- live private multiplayer match flow
- faithful turn-based flick gameplay
- scoring to match public rules unless evidence later forces changes
- digital equivalents for penalties/cards that make sense on mobile
- one default generic brand/theme plus one branded/theme-capable layer
- basic onboarding and settings

## Success criteria
- two remote players can complete a full stable match
- flicking and rebounds feel good on a phone screen
- match rules are deterministic and testable
- branding can be swapped by changing a content/theme pack, not core code
- app is in a plausible App Store submission state

## Risks
- IP / trade dress / trademark risk for exact public clone positioning
- physical rules do not map perfectly to touch controls
- live multiplayer complexity can dominate too early
- board layout evidence may be incomplete or contradictory

## Product stance on fidelity
- Be exact where rules/layout are confirmed.
- Be explicit where rules/layout are inferred.
- Do not silently invent geometry.
- Where physical-only rules do not translate, preserve competitive intent rather than literal form.
